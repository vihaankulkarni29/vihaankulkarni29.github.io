Why This Work Matters

Antimicrobial resistance is projected to cause 10 million deaths annually by 2050. Yet our ability to track and understand resistance mechanisms lags far behind the pace of bacterial evolution.

The solution? Computational genomics. By analyzing bacterial genomes at scale, we can identify resistance genes in hours instead of days, track the spread of resistant strains, and predict which mutations are likely to emerge next.

My work focuses on building practical, open-source tools that democratize access to these powerful analytical capabilities. Because every researcher, regardless of their programming background or institutional resources, should be able to contribute to solving this global health crisis.
