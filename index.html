About Me

As a computational biologist, I build bioinformatics tools that transform biological data into actionable insights — focusing on antimicrobial resistance and comparative genomics. I’m passionate about bridging biology and software engineering to uncover evolutionary patterns that shape resistance mechanisms.

Currently, I work on creating reproducible pipelines for protein structure–function analysis, developing Python-based tools like FastAAExtractor, WildTypeAligner, and NCBI_Genome_Extractor. My broader mission: enabling open, accessible, and data-driven AMR research worldwide.
