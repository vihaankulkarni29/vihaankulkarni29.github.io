My Origin Story

I got into bioinformatics because I kept watching researchers waste entire weeks on tasks that should take hours. During my early work with bacterial genomics, I saw talented microbiologists spending 40+ hours manually extracting sequences from genome assemblies—work I knew could be automated. That's when it clicked: the bottleneck in modern biology isn't generating data, it's making sense of it efficiently.

So I taught myself Python, learned pipeline development with Snakemake, and started building the tools I wished existed when I was doing that tedious manual work. Each project I've built solves a specific problem I've either faced myself or heard about directly from researchers in the trenches. I don't build solutions looking for problems. I build tools that help real people doing real work.

Why Antimicrobial Resistance?

AMR is projected to kill 10 million people annually by 2050—more than cancer. Yet our ability to track and understand how bacteria develop resistance is painfully slow. Traditional lab methods take 3-5 days to identify a resistant infection. By then, patients have already received the wrong antibiotics and potentially spread the resistant strain to others.

Computational genomics can change this timeline dramatically. We can identify resistance genes in hours instead of days, track how resistant strains spread across hospitals and regions, and predict which mutations are likely to emerge next. The technology exists. The sequencing capacity exists. What's missing are tools that work for everyone—not just bioinformaticians at well-funded institutions.

My Mission

That's the gap I'm trying to fill. I build tools for the 90% of researchers who don't have computer science backgrounds, who work in resource-limited settings, who need answers fast and can't spend days debugging code. Clinical microbiologists running surveillance programs. Graduate students in labs without HPC clusters. Researchers in countries where AMR is already a daily crisis.

If we can democratize access to these analytical capabilities, we can build a truly global surveillance network for antimicrobial resistance. That's what drives me.
