Why Antimicrobial Resistance Needs Bioinformatics

Antimicrobial resistance (AMR) is one of the most pressing global health threats, with drug-resistant infections causing hundreds of thousands of deaths annually. Yet, our ability to track, understand, and combat resistance mechanisms is severely limited by the complexity of genomic data.

Traditional microbiological approaches are too slow to keep pace with rapidly evolving pathogens. Computational methods offer a solution—enabling us to analyze thousands of genomes, identify resistance patterns, and predict emerging threats before they spread.

My work focuses on building practical tools that bridge the gap between raw genomic data and actionable insights. By automating tedious analysis workflows and making sophisticated bioinformatics accessible to researchers without extensive programming backgrounds, we can accelerate the pace of AMR research and ultimately save lives.

The future of infectious disease research lies at the intersection of biology and computation. I'm committed to developing open-source solutions that democratize access to powerful analytical tools and help researchers worldwide tackle this critical challenge.
