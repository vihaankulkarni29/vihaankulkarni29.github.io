Research

My research interests, publications, and contributions to bioinformatics and antimicrobial resistance research.

Research Interests

My work focuses on developing computational methods to address critical challenges in infectious disease research, with a particular emphasis on antimicrobial resistance.

Antimicrobial Resistance Genomics

Using computational approaches to track the evolution and spread of antimicrobial resistance genes across bacterial populations. Focus on developing tools for rapid identification and characterization of resistance mechanisms.

Pipeline Development

Building reproducible, scalable bioinformatics pipelines for genomic surveillance. Emphasis on tools that work in resource-limited settings and don't require extensive computational infrastructure.

Machine Learning in Genomics

Exploring AI-driven approaches for predicting resistance phenotypes from genomic data, identifying emerging resistance patterns, and accelerating variant annotation workflows.

Comparative Genomics

Developing methods for large-scale comparative analysis of bacterial genomes to identify regional mutation hotspots and understand geographic patterns in resistance evolution.

Open Science Tools

Creating accessible, well-documented bioinformatics software that democratizes access to sophisticated genomic analysis capabilities for researchers worldwide.

Clinical Bioinformatics

Bridging the gap between genomic data and clinical decision-making through tools that enable rapid, actionable insights for infectious disease management.
